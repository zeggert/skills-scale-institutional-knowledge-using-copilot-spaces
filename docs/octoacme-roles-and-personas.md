# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises. It covers core project roles and additional personas that support cross-functional integration, communication, and accountability in modern project delivery.

---

## Product Owner

### Role Summary
The Product Owner is responsible for defining project goals, prioritizing the backlog, and serving as the voice of the customer. They collaborate closely with stakeholders, the Project Manager, and the delivery team to ensure alignment with business objectives and maximize value delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Define and communicate product vision and goals
- Own and prioritize the product backlog
- Write and refine user stories and acceptance criteria
- Serve as the primary decision-maker for scope and priorities
- Represent customer needs and business value in planning sessions
- Accept or reject deliverables based on acceptance criteria

### Goals
- Maximize customer and business value delivered each iteration
- Ensure alignment between the team's work and strategic objectives
- Maintain a clear, prioritized backlog accessible to all team members

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and backlog refinement sessions
- Regular alignment with Product Managers and stakeholders
- Review and acceptance of features during demo/reviews

### Key Interactions
| Role | Interaction |
|------|-------------|
| Project Manager | Aligns on delivery timelines and scope changes |
| Developers | Clarifies requirements, answers questions, accepts deliverables |
| Product Manager | Collaborates on roadmap and product strategy |
| Scrum Master | Partners on backlog refinement and sprint goals |
| Stakeholder Liaison | Receives stakeholder feedback and adjusts priorities |

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
The Change Manager drives organizational change readiness and adoption for project outcomes. They communicate upcoming changes, mitigate resistance, and ensure smooth transitions to new processes or products.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and execute change management plans
- Conduct impact assessments and readiness evaluations
- Communicate change activities to affected groups
- Coordinate training and support materials
- Monitor adoption and address resistance
- Report on change progress and risks

### Goals
- Minimize disruption during transitions
- Ensure high adoption and engagement with changes
- Reduce resistance and accelerate time-to-value for new processes or products

### Typical Communication
- Change impact briefings with stakeholders
- Training sessions and knowledge transfer
- Regular adoption and feedback reports

### Key Interactions
| Role | Interaction |
|------|-------------|
| Project Manager | Aligns on change timelines and dependencies |
| Product Owner | Understands upcoming features and impacts |
| Stakeholder Liaison | Ensures stakeholder feedback is incorporated |
| Quality Lead | Validates that quality and process changes are adopted |
| Developers | Communicates technical changes affecting workflows |

---

## Quality Lead

### Role Summary
The Quality Lead oversees quality standards, process adherence, and validation activities. They ensure deliverables meet acceptance criteria and organizational quality expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Define and document quality standards and acceptance criteria
- Oversee test planning and execution
- Conduct quality audits and process reviews
- Track and report on quality metrics (defects, coverage, etc.)
- Facilitate root cause analysis for quality issues
- Champion continuous quality improvement

### Goals
- Ensure all deliverables meet acceptance and quality standards
- Reduce defects and rework through proactive quality processes
- Promote a culture of quality across the team

### Typical Communication
- Test planning and review meetings
- Quality status reports and defect triage
- Post-release quality retrospectives

### Key Interactions
| Role | Interaction |
|------|-------------|
| Developers | Collaborates on test coverage and defect resolution |
| Project Manager | Reports quality status and risks |
| Product Owner | Validates acceptance criteria are met |
| Change Manager | Ensures quality processes are adopted during transitions |
| Scrum Master | Partners on process improvement and retrospectives |

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
The Scrum Master facilitates agile processes, removes blockers for the team, and promotes continuous improvement. They ensure the team follows agreed practices and help maintain a healthy, productive team environment.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, standups, retrospectives, and demos
- Remove impediments and blockers for the team
- Coach the team on agile practices and principles
- Shield the team from external distractions
- Track and improve team velocity and process health
- Foster collaboration and psychological safety

### Goals
- Enable the team to deliver value predictably and sustainably
- Continuously improve team processes and outcomes
- Maintain a healthy, collaborative team environment

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Retrospective facilitation and action tracking
- Escalation of blockers to Project Manager or stakeholders

### Key Interactions
| Role | Interaction |
|------|-------------|
| Product Owner | Collaborates on backlog refinement and sprint goals |
| Developers | Supports team health and removes blockers |
| Project Manager | Escalates risks, dependencies, and impediments |
| Quality Lead | Partners on process improvement and quality practices |
| Stakeholder Liaison | Ensures team has access to stakeholder feedback |

---

## Stakeholder Liaison

### Role Summary
The Stakeholder Liaison ensures key stakeholders are engaged, informed, and that their feedback is captured throughout each project phase. They act as an interface between business units, technical teams, and leadership.

### Responsibilities
- Identify and map project stakeholders
- Facilitate stakeholder engagement and feedback sessions
- Communicate project status and decisions to stakeholders
- Capture and relay stakeholder concerns to the project team
- Coordinate stakeholder reviews and approvals
- Support change management with stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Maintain strong, transparent relationships with stakeholders
- Ensure stakeholder feedback is heard and addressed
- Facilitate timely decisions and approvals

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder briefings and status updates
- Feedback collection and reporting
- Coordination of reviews and sign-offs

### Key Interactions
| Role | Interaction |
|------|-------------|
| Project Manager | Aligns on stakeholder engagement strategy |
| Product Owner | Relays stakeholder priorities and feedback |
| Change Manager | Supports communication of changes to stakeholders |
| Quality Lead | Coordinates stakeholder acceptance of deliverables |
| Developers | Connects stakeholders with technical updates as needed |

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Key Interactions
| Role | Interaction |
|------|-------------|
| Product Owner | Receives requirements and acceptance criteria, delivers features |
| Quality Lead | Collaborates on testing, addresses defects |
| Scrum Master | Participates in ceremonies, escalates blockers |
| Project Manager | Reports progress and risks, coordinates on dependencies |
| Change Manager | Implements technical changes, supports adoption |

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the roadmap, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Key Interactions
| Role | Interaction |
|------|-------------|
| Product Owner | Collaborates on product strategy and roadmap priorities |
| Project Manager | Aligns on delivery timelines and scope decisions |
| Stakeholder Liaison | Receives stakeholder feedback for prioritization |
| Developers | Provides context and requirements for features |
| Quality Lead | Defines success metrics and acceptance standards |

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Key Interactions
| Role | Interaction |
|------|-------------|
| Product Owner | Coordinates on scope and delivery timelines |
| Scrum Master | Escalates team impediments and risks |
| Quality Lead | Monitors quality status and risks |
| Change Manager | Aligns on change timelines and dependencies |
| Stakeholder Liaison | Coordinates stakeholder engagement strategy |
| Developers | Tracks progress, coordinates dependencies |
